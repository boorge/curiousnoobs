Iranian Hacker Group APT34 used Saitama Backdoor to Target Jordanian Ministry

Iranian Hacker Group APT34 used Saitama Backdoor to Target Jordanian Ministry On 26th of april 2022, researchers from Malwarebytes labs found a suspicious email that targeted a government official from Jordan's foreign ministry. The email had an Exel file that dropped a backdoor named saitama. After investigation it was found that the attack was executed by Iranian group APT34.

Attack Details
• The attacker pretends to be a government official and sends a malicious email regarding confirmation • The file is named "Confirmation Receive Document[.]xls" •After opening the malicious file the file. The Saitama backdoor starts dling it's work. • The Excel document shows a decoy sheet enclosing the Government of Jordan's coat of arms.

Saitama Backdoor
• The Saitama backdoor abuses DNS protocol for itvs C2 communications, which makes it stealthier than other backdoors. The developer who created it had knowledge about the internal infrastructure because the backdoor had internal IPs and domain names.

Conclusion
The APT34 groups seems to be active and targetting Middle East with it's attacks. The companies are suggested to update their software and anti-malware as well.
